34 /*!
35  \file apps/chem/correlationfactor.h
36  \brief class for regularizing singular potentials in the molecular
37  Hamilton operator
38 
39  \par Introduction
40 
41  The correlation factors are intended to represent the cusps (nuclear or
42  electronic) in the molecular wave function. Their commutator over the
43  kinetic energy operator give a potential operator that cancels the
44  singular potential
45 
46  [T, f12] = U + 1/r12
47  R^-1[T, R] = U_nuc - sum_A Z_A/r1A
48 
49  The regularized potentials U and U_nuc contain two terms each, a local
50  potential, (denoted U2), and a potential that is multiplied with the
51  derivative operator \nabla (denoted U1)
52 
53  U = U1 . (\nabla_1 - \nabla_2) + U2
54  U_nuc = U1 . \nabla + U2
55 
56  with
57 
58  U2 = (\nabla^2 f12)
59  U2 = R^{-1}(\nabla^2 R)
60 
61  \vec U1 = (\vec \nabla f12)
62  \vec U1 = R^{-1}(\vec \nabla R)
63 
64  To construct a nuclear correlation factor write:
65 
66  std::shared_ptr<NuclearCorrelationFactor> nuclear_correlation
67  = create_nuclear_correlation_factor(world,*calc);
68 
69  where calc is an SCF calculation which holds the molecule and the
70  nuclear_corrfac parameter name.
71 */
